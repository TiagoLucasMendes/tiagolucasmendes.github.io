Future Missions

Artemis Program

The Artemis program is intended to reestablish a human presence on the Moon for the first time since the Apollo 17 mission in 1972. The program's long-term goal is to establish a permanent base on the Moon to facilitate the feasibility of human missions to Mars.

Key Components

The Space Launch System (SLS) is a super heavy-lift expendable launch vehicle, which is the main Earth-launch vehicle of the Artemis lunar program.

Orion is a partially reusable spacecraft to be used in the Artemis program capable of supporting six Astronauts in deep space missions.

The Human Landing System (HLS) transports crew from lunar orbit to the lunar surface, acts as a lunar habitat, and then transports the crew back to lunar orbit.

NASA's Gateway is an in-development mini-space station in lunar orbit intended to serve as a communication hub, science laboratory, short-term habitation module, and holding area for rovers and other robots.




Mars sample-return

A Mars sample-return (MSR) mission is a proposed mission to collect rock and dust samples on Mars and return them to Earth. Such a mission would allow more extensive analysis than that allowed by onboard sensors.

Key Points

The Mars 2020 mission landed the Perseverance rover in the Jezero crater in February 2021. It has collected multiple samples, packing them into cylinders for later return in the MSR Campaign.

As a follow up to the Preseverance sample collection, the Mars Return Lander will be launched. It contains a mini hellicopter to collect the samples, a robotic arm to manipulate them and a small rocket to launch them into space.

The then launched Earth Return Orbiter will then rendezvous with the samples in martian orbit and bring them back to Earth for studying.




Europa Clipper

Europa Clipper is an interplanetary mission in development by NASA comprising an orbiter. Planned for launch in October 2024, the spacecraft is being developed to study the Galilean moon Europa through a series of flybys while in orbit around Jupiter

Main Objectives

Ice shell and ocean: Confirm the existence, and characterize the nature, of water within or beneath the ice, and processes of surface-ice-ocean exchange

Composition: Distribution and chemistry of key compounds and the links to ocean composition

Geology: Characteristics and formation of surface features, including sites of recent or current activity.
