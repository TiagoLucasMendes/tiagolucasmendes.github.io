Jet Propulsion Laboratory

Est 1988

Who We Are

The Jet Propulsion Laboratory holds a unique place in the universe. We are a leader in robotic space exploration, sending rovers to Mars, probes into the farthest reaches of the solar system, and satellites to advance understanding of our home planet. The success of JPL’s first spacecraft, the Explorer I satellite, in 1958 helped lift America into the Space Age. Our spacecraft have flown to every planet in the solar system, the Sun, and into interstellar space in a quest to better understand the origins of the universe, and of life. Our missions honor the relentless pursuit of the seeker: Voyager, Curiosity, Cassini, Galileo.

Armstrong Flight Research Center

SET 1999

Who We Are

The Armstrong Flight Research Center, located inside Edwards Air Force Base, also known as the Dryden Flight Research Center (DFRC), is an aeronautical research facility operated by NASA. The center is one of the largest facilities that make up NASA's infrastructure for development projects focused primarily on aviation. On March 1, 2014, the center was renamed in honor of Neil Armstrong, the first man to walk on the moon, changing its name from the Dryden Flight Research Center to the Armstrong Flight Research Center.

Goddard Space Flight Center

SET 2009

Who We Are

The Goddard Space Flight Center (GSFC) is a major United States government facility for scientific research and space missions. It is located in Greenbelt, Maryland, just northeast of Washington, D.C. GSFC is one of NASA's largest installations and is responsible for managing and executing a wide range of space-related missions.GSFC was established in 1959 as NASA's first space flight center. It was named in honor of American rocketry pioneer Dr. Robert H. Goddard. The center's primary mission is to conduct scientific research, develop and operate space systems, and provide support for various NASA missions.

  • Genesis
  • Hélios
  • Ulysses

Johnson Space Center

OUCT 2012

Who We Are

NASA’s Johnson Space Center has served as the iconic setting to some of humankind’s greatest achievements. For more than 60 years, as part of NASA’s nationwide team, Johnson has led the world in human space exploration. Giant Leaps Start Here is Johnson’s center external tagline. It points to the near-term achievements that we will complete with the International Space Station, Commercial Crew and Orion programs, and further highlights our goal of deep space exploration with Gateway and our future lunar endeavors and reminds us of our rich history of exploration.
